The Sacrament of the Eucharist is a sacrament of love, a sign of unity, a bond of charity. It is the source and summit of the Christian life. (CCC, Art 3)

This website aims to help the clueless server familiarise themselves with the various sacred objects required to set up for Mass. Say a lil' prayer and watch this video of how to vest a chalice.

Sacred vessels

Ciborium

Known as a container in latin is a paten in the form of a chalice with a lid. It contains the Sacred Body of our Lord. It is also sometimes used to contain the Hosts reserved in the tabernacle.

Paten

The plate on which the bread (large Host) used by the presider is placed and consecrated during Mass.

Cruets

Vessels to hold the wine and water at the Mass before consecration.

Chalice

A cup used that is set with a base. The vessel is used to hold the Precious Blood of the Lord at Mass.

Lavabo

Contains water for the washing of presider’s hands after the Offeratory and Communion. Always paired with a finger cloth.

Sacred linen

Corporal

A sacred cloth of white linen around 20 inches square upon the chalice, paten and ciboria are placed during Mass. It is usually folded three times to form nine equal squares.

Pall

A square of starch-stiffened linen or cardboard or plastic, set on the chalice to prevent dirt or insects from falling into it.

Finger cloth

Refers to the range of small cloths used by presiders at various times in the liturgy; for drying of presider’s hands after Preparation of the Gifts.

Purificator

A small piece of white absorbent linen folded in three lengthwise and marked with a cross in the centre. Used to wipe the lip of the chalice after the reception of the Precious Blood and to dry the chalice after it is washed at the end of Mass.
